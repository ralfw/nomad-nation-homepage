Get Residency in Bulgaria easily

If you plan to make Bulgaria the country of your homebase or just want to stay longer than you're usually permitted then you should apply for a long term residency. That's easy to do for EU-citizens. We've compiled everything your need below. But should you be a non-EU citizen, not all is lost. Please see our Resident membership plan for our offer to support you in gaining long term residency in Bulgaria.

1

Gather documents

In order to apply for long term residency in Bulgaria you have to appear before the Bulgarian immigration authority and present a couple of documents. This sounds unpleasant, but let us assure you, so far it has been very easy for every EU-citizen - as long as he/she was well prepared. That's what this guide is here for: to help you prepare everything so that the visit to the immigration office becomes a pleasure trip.

Passport

You probably thought of that yourself: You have to bring your passport with you when going to the immigration office.

Proof of Funds

The next easiest thing is to bring "proof of funds". Immigration wants to check that you can sustain yourself while living in Bulgaria.

Fortunately so far it has been easy to convince the authorities of the one's ability to sustain oneself by presenting a valid credit card. Bring a credit card in your name with you and you're fine.

And if you want to be on the safe side, bring a bank statement for the current month with you for a bank account in your name. The balance should be at least 1500€.

Declaration of landlord

For long term residency a place to stay is mandatory. You need to provide evidence that you're not living on the street. The authorities want to be able to reach you at the physical address of a house or apartment you own or are renting.

So what you need to bring is a notarized declaration of your landlord, that he's allowing you to stay at his premise. If you should already own a property, bring a notarized purchase document.

Notarization is very important in many cases in Bulgaria. Fortunately it's not expensive, just sometimes a bit of a hassle. Your landlord should be familiar with that, though.

Health Insurance

Finally you need to prove that you're covered by some health insurance.

If you've been member of a public health insurance in your EU home country, then that should not be a problem at all. Just see to that you have your health insurance card with the European Union symbol at hand. Here's an example from Germany, that's why it say "DE" in the circle of stars. For your country it should look similar on the back of your card:

Should you not have such a card or are not insured in the public health system in an EU country, then you can obtain a private health insurance in Bulgaria. It has worked in the past with such a contract valid for one year which should be enough if you plan to open a business in Bulgaria and then get health insured through that. Of course all Nomad Nation membership plans provide you with Bulgarian public health insurance.

2

Fill in the application form

The actual application is submitted using a special form you can get at the immigration office. But to wait with filling it out until you're in front of the clerk is a sure fire method for stress. Better you do that in the comfort of your home.

Here's how to do that:

1. Download the application form for the immigration office relevant for you. If your immigration office is not listed, please let us know.

Blagoevgrad form

2. It's easiest you fill out the form on your computer. Open the PDF with a program which let's you fill in PDF form fields. On a Mac just double-click and use the preview app; on Windows it should work equally easy. For details on how to fill out the form see, get instructions here.

3. If you left blank the form fields for the cyrillic transliterations of your data given in latin script, find someone who can help with that - or book our convenient service for a small fee. We'll also check if all your checkmarks are plausibel.

4

Go to immigration office

Once you've compiled all the documents and made sufficient copies go to the relevant immigration office.

Blagoevgrad

ul. "Vanyusha Valchuk" 6, 2700 Blagoevgrad Center, Blagoevgrad

Get Directions

Enter through the gate here. Inside turn left, enter the main room, turn to the left again. The relevant clerk for you is the first one on the left. The ladies there are very friendly - but don't speak English. When you present your application form and have all documents and copies at hand, though, they should process you without many questions.

  • It's best you go there in there early in the morning, e.g. 8:30.
  • If all goes well you can pick up your residency permit after 15:00 at the same day. This small white card is important! Don't loose it; keep it safe like your passport.
  • Be prepared to pay a small sum for the application right there at the cashier's.
  • Opt for express processing of your application. That means you can pick up your Bulgarian ID card 3 days later at the same office.

We wish you all the best for your application!
