Tourist

The Tourist membership serves multiple purposes. It's Nomad Nation's entry-level plan.

Say, you're only serving one client most of the time - in some countries that might even be called "bogus self-employment" - and only have negligible expenses, then the Tourist provides you with a cost effective, low effort, peace of mind solution for your business in Bulgaria. As a Tourist member you're billing your client with Nomad Nation as the invoice issuer, a full fledged company.

Or, say, you're in the process of finding homebase for yourself and your solopreneur business. You don't know if you want to settle in Bulgaria or keep searching. Currently you're a perpetual traveller and not paying taxes anywhere. Then the Tourist membership is an option to make your income "more official". Because once you settle somewhere (or even return to your home country) authorities could ask, where you had been paying taxes in the meantime. To answer with "nowhere" often does not make them very happy. But answering with "in Bulgaria" and showing them your tax declarations and even social security payments could make a very positive difference.

We view the Tourist membership more as a temporary and/or occasional membership. You use it while in a transitory phase of your business.

How it works

As a Tourist member you're signing so called civil contracts with Nomad Nation for each B2B client you're serving. (And one additional contract if you also should do B2C business.) You're not employed by Nomad Nation, but contracted. This keeps the relationship comparatively loose - and thus easy to enter and easy to leave.

Nomad Nation is your client facing business entity in whose name you're writing invoices. But you'll be the sole beneficiary of the revenue. When your client pays you the money arrives on your member bank account.

Pros

No company registration required

Becoming a Tourist member is so easy, you can do it while being at a coworking BBQ - and invoice your client the next day from Bulgaria through Nomad Nation.

Business bank account

Nomad Nation will provide you with a business bank account since you're using its Business-as-a-Service platform for billing your clients.

Incl. VAT number

Your clients will receive invoices from Nomad Nation which is VAT registered. Even as a natural person without a registered business in Bulgaria you can thus interact professionally with B2B clients.

Tax handled for you

You're not employed by Nomad Nation, but just a contractual partner. Nevertheless you'll be paying taxes and social security in Bulgaria - and Nomad Nation will handle that for you. No worries.

Why is that a pro statement? Because having a track record of tax payments and social security contributions is a good thing when talking to authorities all over the world. In addition we think that it's respectful to a country which you're using as a business platform, to pay your dues. Fortunately those are very low in Bulgaria.

Easy to cancel

You can cancel your membership at any time. Your civil contracts are just discontinued and you withdraw all your funds from your member bank account.

Cons

No assets / expenses

To make the Tourist membership easy to enter and leave it's necessary to keep the management effort low. That's why you cannot submit expenses and not accumulate assets as a Tourist member. But that should not be a problem for a comparatively short sojourn in Nomad Nation while your transitioning your business to its final destination - which might be another Nomad Nation membership plan.

Limited transactions

To keep the price for the Tourist plan low, unfortunately the number of invoices you can send each month per civil contract has to be limited, too.

Of course this is not attractive if your business is growing and buzzing with new clients. But we think that's ok, because then you simply switch to another membership plan better suitable for you. No need to endure the limitations of the Tourist - and at the same time no need to burden the Tourist plan with cost increasing flexibility.

Pricing

59 € /month

This includes the accounting services for handling 1 "active civil contract" per month plus the membership fee. Nomad Nation calls that a transaction and encompasses tax declaration and social security handling. An "active civil contract" is one you wrote up to 3 invoices for in that month. The monthly fee thus does not dependent on the number of clients you have (aka civil contracts). It's just limited to billing one of those clients in a given month. Remember: Should you do B2C business, then all invoices do different consumer clients are part of the same transaction and count towards the invoice limit.

Get started

Additional transactions: 35€/transaction

If you write invoices to different clients in a month, i.e. are active on several of your civil contracts, then for each additional transaction you'd have to pay a fixed fee.

This fee also applies if you should write more than 3 invoices pertaining to a single civil contract; this usually is only relevant in B2C scenarios.
