Resident

The main scenario the Resident membership is designed for is: a non-EU citizen wants to get long term residency in Bulgaria. If you're a UK citizen thinking about moving to Bulgaria that should be highly interesting for you; but if you're a US citizen or from Canada or Australia or Japan trying to get a foothold in Europe that's relevant for you, too.

But due to the way the membership works it might also be an option for solopreneurs from the EU with low to medium revenue and more than 25% expenses.

How it works

The Resident membership is very special: Nomad Nation is helping you as a solopreneur by employing you. That might sound paradoxical, but it's true: Only through hiring you it will be possible for you to easily apply for long term residency as a non-EU citizen. Also as an employee you'll benefit from much reduced bureaucratic overhead for your business.

But rest assured: Once you've become an employee Nomad Nation will leave you alone as much as possible. You do your own business, you stay independent in your client relationships. It's just that the revenue you generate will be payed out to you as a salary.

As a Resident member you'll pay yourself the minimum required Bulgarian salary of some 400€/mo in total (including income tax and all social security contributions). This much revenue you'll have to make at least (plus the monthly membership fee; see below) - and to ensure the ability to pay yourself this salary your bank account balance needs to be at least 500€.

Whatever you want to take out of your business on top of your minimum salary you can pay yourself as a bonus in addition to the minimum salary. (The bonus will of course also be taxed and you pay social security from it.) You can decide yourself each month what your bonus should be; no need to ask a manager, just because you're employed. You profit is yours to keep.

Being employed also means, you'll be paying social security and thus are covered by the Bulgarian health insurance.

Pros

Residency for non-EU citizens

The Resident membership is Nomad Nations unique package to help you getting long term residency in Bulgaria if you are a non-EU citizen (and don't have long term residency in another EU country already). In that case a visa type D is required and that's not so easy to come by.

If you happen to be fluent on a B1 level in Bulgarian or about to marry a Bulgarian (or EU resident with Bulgarian long term residency) or want to set up an expensive representative office in Bulgaria... then you don't need the help of this membership package.

But should you not, then Nomad Nation can put you on the roadt to a Blue Card by employing you.

You'd be formally employed by Nomad Nation and earn a monthly salary - but in reality you'd of course continue doing your own business. Nomad Nation would just be your platform to bill your clients from.

Once you have the Blue Card and have been employed for 5 years in Bulgaria, you can apply for long term residency. Or maybe one of the other options has become available to you in the meantime...

No company registration required

If you're "bureaucracy averse" the Nomad Nation Resident membership is perfect for you. You don't have to deal with authorities, you don't have to think about civil contracts when taking up new clients. Just sign the employment contract and Nomad Nation will do your taxes, and your payroll.

Usually you're operational after 3 days and can start invoicing your clients.

Incl. VAT number

Nomad Nation is VAT registered and so are you when doing business on Nomad Nation as your platform.

Tax handled for you

When employed by Nomad Nation we're taking care of your tax/social security payment. Assuming you're not a tax resident elsewhere anymore that makes it very easy for you.

Some solopreneurs prefer to do their taxes and monthly accounting themselves. But that's not really an option in Bulgaria as a foreigner. But Nomad Nation is working with experts who'll take good care of you. Just focus on your business.

Business bank account

As a Resident member you'll get your own business bank account to receive payments from your customers on, and pay any business expenses from. This makes it easy for Nomad Nation to keep track of your transactions for bookkeeping purposes and not bother you with tax/social security payments.

Cons

Limited expenses

As a Resident member you're doing your business independently. But at the same time you're running it on a platform shared by other members. We call that autonomous employment instead of self-employment.

Unfortunately that requires us to limit your freedom somewhat for the benefit of the whole: you can only submit expenses up to a total of 2.500€/per month.

Limited assets

Some expenses of your business might count as assets to be written off over the course of several years, e.g. a laptop computer.

As a Resident member you are allowed assets in the total amount of max. 5.000€ at any time "in your books".

Pricing

99 € /month

The fee covers the monthly accounting/payroll service plus the Nomad Nation membership fee.

When I first came to Bansko from Germany it was not easy to decide what the best option would be to set up my solopreneur business. With my girlfriend in the same situation as me I did a lot of research - and finally settled on my own EOOD. This decision was not wrong given our prior experience in Germany and the information we had compiled. But it turned out not to deliver the results I expected. Plus, it turned out the expenses I was used to deduct in Germany weren't nearly as high in Bulgaria.

The EOOD worked, but I had hoped for a smoother experience.

That's what I've now got as a Nomad Nation Resident member! Even though the Bulgarian long term residency was not a problem for me, the Nomad Nation Resident plan is making things easier. I can focus more on my business.

Had there been a service like Nomad Nation when we came to Bulgaria, we could have much stress and money. That's why I co-founded Nomad Nation with Daniel: to make it easier for other solopreneurs to move to Bulgaria and get their business going.
